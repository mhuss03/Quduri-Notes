Iqālah — Cancellation of Sale

باب الإقالة

Mutual cancellation of a sale contract by agreement of both buyer and seller.

Arabic text

جائزة في البيع بمثل الثمن الأول فإن شرط أقل منه أو أكثر فالشرط باطل ويرد مثل الثمن الأول · وهي فسخ في حق المتعاقدين بيع جديد في حق غيرهما في قول أبي حنيفة

Core concept

What is Iqālah?

Mutual cancellation of a sale contract by agreement of both buyer and seller, returning each party to their original position. Both parties must agree.

Dual legal nature (Abū Ḥanīfa)

For the two parties

Treated as faskh (rescission)

Simply unwinds the original sale

No new transaction

For third parties

Treated as a new sale

Protects third-party rights

e.g. shuf3a (pre-emption) can be triggered

Price condition

✓ Valid

Iqālah at the original price (al-thaman al-awwal). The sale is cancelled and original price/goods returned.

✗ Condition void

Any stipulation of more or less — the condition is bāṭil but the iqālah itself proceeds at the original price.

Effect of destruction (halāk)

Thaman destroyed Iqālah valid

Money is fungible — return the mithil (equivalent). Iqālah remains valid.

Mabī' destroyed Iqālah void

If the sold item is destroyed, iqālah cannot proceed — a substitute could unfairly favour one party.

Partial destruction of mabī'

Iqālah is valid for the remaining portion only. Example: some fruit on a tree destroyed → iqālah on the surviving fruit only.

Rule-by-rule breakdown

1 · Permissibility

Iqālah is jā'iza (permitted) in sale contracts.

2 · Original price only

Must be at the original price (al-thaman al-awwal). Any condition of more or less is bāṭil — iqālah still proceeds at original price.

3 · Dual characterisation (Abū Ḥanīfa)

Between parties: faskh (rescission). Toward third parties: new sale — allowing shuf3a rights to be triggered.

4 · Thaman destroyed

Does not prevent iqālah. Thaman is fungible — the mithil (equivalent) is returned instead.

5 · Mabī' destroyed

Prevents iqālah. The actual item must be returnable; a substitute could create inequity.

6 · Partial destruction of mabī'

Iqālah valid for the remaining undestroyed portion only. Applied proportionally.

Murābaḥa & Tawliya

باب المرابحة والتولية

Cost-plus sale and at-cost transfer — the rules of disclosed-price transactions.

Arabic text

المرابحة: نقل ما ملكه بالعقد الأول بالثمن الأول مع زيادة ربح · والتولية: نقل ما ملكه بالعقد الأول بالثمن الأول من غير زيادة ربح

Core definitions

Murābaḥa المرابحة

Reselling at the original price plus a disclosed profit. The buyer knows exactly what the seller paid and the markup being added.

Tawliya التولية

Reselling at exactly the original price — no profit, no loss. A transfer at cost price only.

Validity condition — 3iwad must be fungible (mithli)

Neither murābaḥa nor tawliya is valid unless the original consideration is fungible. Non-fungible goods do not qualify.

Adding costs to rās al-māl

Permitted additional costs

Wages of the dyer, bleacher, embroiderer, spinner, and transport cost may be added to the capital base. The seller must say "it cost me X" (qāma 3alayya bi-kadhā) — not "I bought it for X" (ishtaraytuhu), which would misrepresent the base cost.

Possession (qabd) before resale

Movable goods qabd required

Cannot resell a movable item until you have taken possession.

Real estate AH + AY: permitted

Abū Ḥanīfa and Abū Yūsuf allow selling real estate before qabd. Imām Muḥammad: not permitted.

Thaman & price adjustments

Trading thaman before qabd: valid

Seller may transact with the debt price before receiving it.

Price/goods adjustments: valid

Buyer may increase price; seller may add to goods or reduce price. Rights attach to the agreed adjusted terms.

Deferred payment — any due debt can be deferred, except loans (qard)

If a seller postpones an immediate debt for a known period, it becomes deferred (mu'ajjal). Exception: a loan — postponing it is not valid; it remains repayable on demand.

Deception (khiyāna) — three scholar positions

Imām MuḥammadNo price reduction in either — buyer has choice (take or return) in both

Ribā — The Prohibition of Usury

باب الربا

The 3illah (legal cause) of ribā and the rulings on tafāḍul and nasā'.

Arabic text

الربا محرم · كل مكيل أو موزون إذا بيع بجنسه متفاضلا فالعلة فيه الكيل مع الجنس أو الوزن مع الجنس

Ribā is ḥarām

Any measured (makkīl) or weighed (mawzūn) item sold against its own kind with excess (tafāḍul) is ribā. The 3illah is: kayl + same jins, OR wazn + same jins.

The two prohibitions

Tafāḍul — inequality of amount

Exchanging 1kg wheat for 1.5kg wheat — the excess quantity is ribā. Even quality differences don't justify it: good dates for bad dates must still be equal by measure.

Nasā' — deferral of delivery

Delay in receiving one side when both items share the genus + measurement 3illah. Both prohibitions apply together when both conditions are present.

The four combinations

Both conditions → tafāḍul ḥarām + nasā' ḥarām

e.g. wheat:wheat, gold:gold — same jins + same 3illah. Must be equal in measure and both handed over in the same session.

Neither condition → tafāḍul ḥalāl + nasā' ḥalāl

e.g. wheat:gold — different jins and different 3illah. Both excess and deferral are permitted.

One condition only → tafāḍul ḥalāl, nasā' ḥarām

e.g. wheat:barley (same 3illah, different jins). Excess OK, but deferral forbidden. Also gold:silver (same wazn 3illah, different jins).

Fixed commodities vs custom (3urf)

Wheat, barley, dates, salt → always makkīl (from prophetic text), even if people stop using measures. Gold, silver → always mawzūn. Anything not mentioned follows local custom (3urf).

Ṣarf — currency exchange exception

When both sides are gold/silver: both must be physically received (qabd) in the same session. For other ribā goods, only ta3yīn (identification) is needed — not simultaneous receipt.

Exceptions — no ribā applies

Master & slave

No ribā applies — the slave's wealth is effectively the master's property.

Muslim in dār al-ḥarb

No ribā between a Muslim and a ḥarbī in enemy territory.

Salam — Forward Sale

باب السلم

Advance-payment sale: price paid upfront, goods delivered at a future date.

Arabic text

السلم جائز في المكيلات والموزونات والمعدودات التي لا تتفاوت كالجوز والبيض وفي المذروعات · ولا يجوز السلم في الحيوان ولا في أطرافه

What is salam?

A sale where the full price (ra's al-māl) is paid upfront and the described goods (musallam fīh) are delivered at a known future date. Essentially buying a described commodity now for future delivery.

Valid categories

Permitted in

Measured goods, weighed goods, uniformly-counted goods (e.g. walnuts, eggs), goods measured by length (cloth), and bricks with a known mould size.

Not permitted in

Animals, animal parts, hides by count, firewood by bundle, fresh fodder by bundle, gems, or beads — too much variation to describe precisely.

General principle

Anything whose description can be controlled (ḍabṭ al-ṣifa) and quantity known (ma3rifat al-miqdār) is valid for salam. Anything failing either test is not.

Must be deferred — no spot salam

Salam must have a known future delivery date (ajal ma3lūm). Immediate salam is not valid. Also forbidden: salam by a specific person's instrument, from a named village's crop, or from a specific tree.

Ra's al-māl — received before parting

The capital must be fully handed over before buyer and seller separate. No transacting in the ra's al-māl or musallam fīh before qabd. Shirka and tawliya in the musallam fīh before qabd: not permitted.

Scholar difference (AH: 7 conditions · AY + Muḥammad: 5)

AH additionally requires: stating the ra's al-māl quantity, and naming the delivery location if transport is needed. AY and Muḥammad: if capital is physically present, naming it is unnecessary; delivery defaults to place of contract.

Abū Ḥanīfa's 7 conditions

  • Known genus (jins ma3lūm)Type of commodity specified — e.g. "wheat" not just "grain".
  • Known variety (naw3 ma3lūm)Specific variety stated — e.g. "white wheat" vs "red wheat".
  • Known quality (ṣifa ma3lūma)Grade or quality described — e.g. "fine quality" or "second grade".
  • Known delivery term (ajal ma3lūm)A specific known future delivery date. Immediate delivery is not valid for salam.
  • Known quantity of ra's al-mālIf capital is measured/weighed/counted, its quantity must be specified. (AY + Muḥammad: not required if physically present.)
  • Named delivery place (if transport needed)If goods require carrying and expense, delivery location must be stated. (AY + Muḥammad: defaults to place of contract.)
  • Ra's al-māl received before partingFull capital payment handed over before parties physically separate.

Ṣarf — Currency Exchange

باب الصرف

Exchange where both counter-values are from the genus of monetary metals (gold/silver).

Arabic text

الصرف هو البيع إذا كان كل واحد من العوضين من جنس الأثمان · لا بد من قبض العوضين قبل الافتراق

Ṣarf — currency exchange

A sale where both sides of the exchange are from the genus of thamans (monetary metals: gold and/or silver).

Same genus vs different genus

Same genus (gold:gold or silver:silver)

Must be equal weight — no tafāḍul. Quality and workmanship (ṣiyāgha) make no difference. Both sides received before the parties separate.

Different genus (gold:silver)

Tafāḍul permitted — 10g gold for 200g silver is fine. But mutual receipt (taqābuḍ) before parting is still obligatory.

Parting before qabd = contract void

If parties separate before receiving either or both counter-values, the contract is void (bāṭil). No transacting with the thaman before receiving it.

Mujāzafa — bulk gold:silver exchange

Selling gold for silver without specifying exact weights is permitted, since they are different genera and tafāḍul is allowed.

Alloy coins — majority rule

Silver majority → treated as silver. Gold majority → treated as gold. Base metal (ghish) majority → not treated as dirhams/dinars; tafāḍul between them is permitted.

Coins become obsolete (kasād) — three opinions

AH: sale void. AY: buyer pays value at time of sale. Muḥammad: buyer pays value at last time coins circulated.

Partially received silver vessel

Contract valid for paid portion, void for the rest. Vessel becomes jointly owned (mushāraka). If part is then claimed by a third party (istiḥqāq): buyer may take the remainder at its proportional price, or return everything.

Mixed-sale worked examples

Jewelled sword: value 100 dh, silver decoration = 50 dh — buyer pays 50 dh

Valid — the payment is allocated to the silver decoration. If they part without mutual exchange: the decoration contract is void; the sword contract is valid only if the sword can be separated without damage.

2 dirhams + 1 dinar for 2 dinars + 1 dirham

Valid — each genus matches against its own genus first. The crossing between genera is resolved by pairing like with like, then the remainder trades cross-genus (tafāḍul ḥalāl).

11 dirhams for 10 dirhams + 1 dinar

Valid — 10 dirhams offset 10 dirhams (equal), and the remaining 1 dirham is the price for the 1 dinar (cross-genus: tafāḍul ḥalāl).

2 good dirhams + 1 worn dirham (ghalla) for 1 good dirham + 2 worn dirhams

Valid — total count is equal (3 for 3). Quality differences within the same genus do not affect validity as long as quantities match.

The pairing principle

In any mixed exchange, dirhams match dirhams and dinars match dinars first. Any surplus in one genus is the price for the surplus in the other (cross-genus tafāḍul ḥalāl). This prevents hidden ribā from slipping in through bundled transactions.

Fulūs (copper coins) rules

Circulating fulūs (nāfiqa)

Sale valid without specifying individual coins — ta3yīn not required. Market rate determines quantity.

Obsolete fulūs (kāsida)

Sale not valid unless specific coins are identified and handed over (ta3yīn required).

Currency goes obsolete — scholar comparison

ScholarRuling when currency becomes obsolete
Abū ḤanīfaSale void (bāṭil) — currency no longer valid
Abū YūsufSale valid — buyer pays equivalent value at time of sale
Imām MuḥammadSale valid — buyer pays value at last circulation date

The "half dirham in fulūs" problem

"Half in fulūs, half minus one grain as a half-coin" — money-changer problem

AH: Entire transaction is corrupt (fāsid) — the "minus one grain" clause creates uncertainty tainting the whole contract.
AY + Muḥammad: Fulūs portion valid, silver half-coin portion void only.

"Half a dirham in fulūs and a half-coin minus one grain" (stated separately)

Valid for all scholars — the two legs are stated separately and not linked with a corrupting internal condition. Same elements, valid structure.
